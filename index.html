My Top Favourite Movies

From childhood to adulthood, all of these films have a special place in my heart

Little Women (2019) - Directed By Greta Gerwig

Derived from the book of the same title by Lousia May Alcott, this movie is adaptation. Telling a tale of four sisters, this is a coming of age movie about their lives during the 1860s in America. The four sisters tackle their own lives and their family lives, finding the meaning of what it means to be a woman.

Bullet Train (2022) - Directed by David Leitch

Bullet Train is an adaptation from a novel by Kōtarō Isaka. The movie is about a man, nicknamed "Ladybug", who goes on a bullet train in Japan to help deliver a suitcase from the start to the end of the train line. When the suitcase gets misplaced in the wrong hands, Ladybug finds himself trying to get back the suitcase and to survive the journey by not getting himself killed by passangers who also want the suitcase.

The Grand Budapest Hotel (2014) - Directed by Wes Anderson

The Grand Budapest Hotel is an original movie directed by Wes Anderson. This movie is about a concerige named Gustave and a lobby boy named Zero who became unlikely friends while managing a ski resort named the "Grand Budapest Hotel." However, when Gustave gets to know Zero and becomes a prime murder suspect of one of his lovers, Gustave tries his best to defend his innocence while making sure Zero is safe.

Spider-Man: Into the Spiderverse (2018) - Directed by Peter Ramsey, Bob Persichetti & Rodney Rothman

This particular movie is an animated movie from Sony Pictures Animation. It tells a story about a kid named Miles Morales who discovers his spider powers after being bitten by a radioactive spider after witnessing the main Spider-Man dying in his own arms. While mourning for the previous Spider-Man a portal dimension opened. He soon discovers other individuals like him have come through the portal. With their help, Miles forms alliances with them to help hunt down the main person who caused the original Spider-man to get killed.

Howl's Moving Castle (2004) - Directed by Hayao Miyazaki

Howl's Moving Castle is adapted from a novel by Diana Wynne Jones. This animated movie is about a girl named Sophie Hatter who runs a hat business and becomes friends with a wizard by the name of Howl who lives in a moving castle. After an evil witch discovers about Sophie and Howl's interactions, the witch turns Sophie into an old woman out of spite. Sophie and Howl now must work together to undo the witches spell before it's too late.

The Wild Robot (2024) - Directed by Chris Sanders

The Wild Robot is a book turned movie about an intelligent robot named Roz who finds themselves stranded on a remote island surrounded by wildlife. After being abandoned by their creator, Roz meets the wildlife living there of all sorts from land, aquatic and flying who become cautious of Roz's self. After becoming good friends with a lonely fox and eventually raising a small bird of their own, she discovers what it means to love, to survive and what family means.

Paperman (2012) - Directed by John Kahrs

As a bonus "film", I wanted to showcase one of my favourite short films. Paperman is black and white short film about a man who meets a woman who he met while waiting for his train. The man says goodbye to the woman while she boards her train, but eventually meets her again as he discovered she worked across from him in a different building across the street. To stay connected to her, the guy makes a paper airplane to get her attention and does so everyday. Eventually after some chasing, they do meet up again at the end of the film and it's a happy ending.
